Brainy Child Guide is a **premium parenting app ($47/month)** that teaches parents science-based communication techniques to handle challenging child behaviors. The app targets parents in the **UK and US markets** who are dealing with intense emotions, defiance, or attention difficulties in their children.

## üí° Core Purpose

The app helps parents **transform high-conflict moments** (tantrums, bedtime resistance, screen time battles, etc.) into **calm, productive interactions** using neuroscience-backed scripts called **NEP (Nonviolent Escalation Prevention)**.

Instead of yelling, threatening, or giving in, parents learn to use specific 3-phrase sequences that work WITH their child's brain, not against it.

## üß† The Three Brain Profiles

Every child in the app is categorized into one of three neurodivergent-friendly profiles:

### 1. **INTENSE**
- Highly emotional, perfectionist tendencies
- Big reactions to small disappointments
- Difficulty with emotional regulation
- Brain characteristics: Hyperactive amygdala, overactive default mode network

### 2. **DISTRACTED**
- Attention difficulties, sensory sensitivity
- Forgets instructions, loses focus easily
- Struggles with transitions and routines
- Brain characteristics: Weak working memory, sensory processing challenges

### 3. **DEFIANT**
- Extreme autonomy needs, oppositional behavior
- Resists authority, tests boundaries constantly
- Control battles over everything
- Brain characteristics: Hyperactive anterior cingulate cortex (autonomy threat detector)

## üìù How the NEP Framework Works

Every script in the app follows a **3-phrase structure**:

1. **CONNECTION (Phrase 1)**: Name what the child is feeling/experiencing without judgment
   - *Example: "Your brain won't let go of today."*

2. **VALIDATION (Phrase 2)**: Acknowledge their want AND state the boundary
   - *Example: "You keep replaying what happened, AND your body needs rest to handle tomorrow better."*

3. **COMMAND (Phrase 3)**: Give a clear choice or directive
   - *Example: "Tell me the one sentence version, or write it down. You pick, two minutes."*
