  - Lower frustration tolerance
  - More difficulty with transitions

### Why Screens Dysregulate

**Dopamine Flooding:**
- Screens provide constant novel stimuli
- Brain releases dopamine at unnatural levels
- Natural rewards (play, reading, conversation) feel inadequate
- Creates tolerance â†’ needs MORE to feel same effect

**Attention Fragmentation:**
- Average shot length in children's media: 3-4 seconds
- Brain adapts to rapid switching
- Sustained attention becomes difficult
- Real life feels "slow" and "boring"

**Emotional Regulation Bypass:**
- Screens provide instant distraction from discomfort
- Child never learns to tolerate frustration
- Boredom = immediate screen reach
- No practice with difficult emotions

**Sleep Disruption:**
- Blue light suppresses melatonin
- Content keeps brain activated
- Poor sleep = poor regulation next day
- Cycle of tiredness â†’ screens â†’ worse sleep

### The Reset Protocol (Based on Dr. Victoria Dunckley's Research)

**"Electronic Fast" Findings:**
- 4-6 weeks of minimal screens
- 80% of children showed improved behavior
- Better sleep, mood, focus
- Reduced aggression and defiance
- Works even without diagnosis

### Implementation Strategies

**1. The Cold Turkey Approach**
- **Best for:** High dependency, severe dysregulation
- **How:** Remove all recreational screens at once
- **Expect:** 3-5 days of intense protest
- **Timeline:** Major improvement by day 7-10

**2. The Gradual Reduction**
- **Best for:** Moderate use, younger children
- **How:** Reduce by 15-30 mins per week
- **Expect:** Less intense but prolonged adjustment
- **Timeline:** 3-4 weeks to new baseline

**3. The Time-Limited Approach**
- **Best for:** Maintenance after reset
- **How:** Strict time window (e.g., 4-5pm only)
- **Rules:** Timer ends = screen ends (no negotiation)
- **Key:** Adult controls off switch, not child

### Critical Success Factors

**Environment Design:**
- Remove visible screens from common areas
- Charge devices in parent's room
- No screens at meals or in car
- Have alternative activities readily available

**Parent Behavior:**
- Model low-screen behavior yourself
- Don't use phone during child time
- Prepare for the extinction burst
- Commit to at least 2 weeks

**Replacement Activities:**
- High engagement (not passive)
- Outdoors when possible
- Sensory (play dough, water, sand)
- Social (games, conversation)
- Creative (art, building, pretend play)

- "Extinction burst" is real
- Day 2-3 usually hardest

- You don't need their buy-in
- You don't need to negotiate
- You are the parent
- This is for their brain health

### Scripts for Screen Removal

**Announcing the Change:**
> "We're making a change in our family. For the next [time period], we won't be using [device] for [shows/games]. This isn't a punishment - it's because I love you and want to help your brain."

**During the Meltdown:**
> "I hear that you're really upset. Screens are done for now. I'm here with you."

**When They Beg:**
> "I know you wish you could watch. The answer is no."

**For the "I'm Bored":**
> "Boredom is your brain looking for something to do. You'll figure it out."

**When They Blame You:**
> "You can be mad at me. I'm still keeping this limit because it's good for you."
