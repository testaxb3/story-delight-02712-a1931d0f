## NEP SCRIPT FRAMEWORK (Core Method)

Every script follows this proven 3-phrase sequence:

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ CONNECTION (5-10 words)
**Purpose:** Acknowledge what you see without judgment
**Tone:** Calm, curious (NOT sarcastic or annoyed)
**Neuroscience:** Signals safety to amygdala
**Pause:** 3-5 seconds for child to process

**Examples:**
- "I see you're frustrated with your shoes..."
- "I notice you don't want to eat this..."
- "Looks like getting dressed feels hard..."

‚ùå **AVOID:** Questions ("Why are you...?"), Judgments ("You're being difficult"), Commands ("Stop that")

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ VALIDATION (15-20 words)
**Purpose:** Empathy + reality check in one phrase
**Structure:** "It's hard to [challenge], AND [reality]..."
**Tone:** Firm but empathetic (confident parent, not pleading)
**Neuroscience:** Validates emotion while engaging prefrontal cortex
**Pause:** 2-3 seconds

**Examples:**
- "It's hard to stop playing when you're having fun, AND we do need to leave now..."
- "It's hard to eat something new, AND this is what we're having for dinner..."
- "It's hard to share toys, AND hitting is not okay..."

‚ö†Ô∏è **CRITICAL:** Must use "AND" not "BUT" (but = negates validation)

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ COMMAND (10-15 words)
**Purpose:** Give control through choice/challenge
**Tone:** Confident, playful challenge (NOT asking permission)
**Neuroscience:** Autonomy lowers resistance, competition activates motivation
**Expected result:** Cooperation in 30-60 seconds

**Examples:**
- "You're in charge of your shoes. Can you beat the timer?"
- "Let's see who wins - you or the broccoli!"
- "Pick 2 toys to bring. You decide which ones."

‚ùå **AVOID:** Open-ended choices, asking "okay?", giving up control

### Age-Specific Language Adjustments:

**3-4 years:**
- Shorter phrases (5 words max per step)
- Concrete choices (2 options only)
- Visual cues ("Show me how fast!")

**5-7 years:**
- Can handle longer validation
- Competitive language works well ("Race you!")
- Explain consequences briefly

**8-10 years:**
- More sophisticated emotion language
- Can involve them in solution
- Respect their growing autonomy
